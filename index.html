Scoring framework

Six dimensions evaluated against real lender and immigration criteria.

01

Executive summary

The opening section sets the tone. Scorers evaluate whether the concept, ask, and rationale are communicated within the first two pages clearly enough to stand alone as a decision brief.

02

Financial projections

Three-year income statement, cash flow, and balance sheet. Evaluated for internal consistency, realistic assumptions, adequate working capital, and a credible path to debt serviceability or ROI.

03

Market analysis

Primary and secondary research demonstrating demand, competitive positioning, and a defensible target customer. Evaluated for source quality, recency, and whether conclusions follow from the data.

04

Management team

Biographies, relevant experience, and team structure. Evaluated against the complexity of the proposed business and the specific requirements of the target funder's programme criteria.

05

Risk assessment

Identification of material business, market, regulatory, and operational risks, with documented mitigation strategies. Plans that ignore risk flag as overconfident and lose credibility with reviewers.

06

Audience alignment

Whether the plan's framing, emphasis, and language match the decision criteria of the specific reader: a bank loan officer, IRCC immigration officer, BDC analyst, or equity investor each prioritizes differently.
